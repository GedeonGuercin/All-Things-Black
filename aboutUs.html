Princeton Caribbean Connection (PCC)

PCC aims to provide all Princeton students a means for celebrating and learning about the Caribbean.

Princeton Black Men's Association (PBMA)

PBMA promotes and enhances the Black male experience on campus through a range of academic, social, and service events.

Princeton Ethiopian and Eritrean Students Association (PEESA)

Dedicated to bringing some of Ethiopia and Eritrea to Princeton through various cultural, educational, as well as service-oriented events.

DoroBucci

Princeton's premier African dance group which seeks to celebrate and understand African culture through the performing arts.

Princeton African Students Association (PASA)

PASA raises awareness on Africa & Africa-related issues and explores/celebrates the diversity of African cultures to foster unity/friendship amongst members.

Princeton Association of Black Women (PABW)

PABW celebrates, unifies, and educates the community of black women on the Princeton campus.

National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE)

NSBE promotes academic excellence through the form of tutoring, mentoring and incentive programs.

The Black Pre-Med Society of Princeton

The Black Pre-Medical Society of Princeton University seeks to provide the necessary support and resources catered to the specific needs of Black students as they ascend towards medical school.

Society of African Internationals at Princeton (SAIP)

The Society of African Internationals at Princeton seeks to provide a community for students born and raised on the African continent and those with African heritage to share and celebrate the diversity of their origins with each other.

TruThursday

A subset of Christian Union Nova, TruThursday exists to reveal and exemplify the Gospel while celebrating African, African American and Caribbean cultures. We aim to provide a place of Tru praise, worship, and teaching of God's word for students of all backgrounds.

PU Steel Ensemble: Tiger Chunes

Chunes, the Princeton University Steel Ensemble aims to make Caribbean culture more visible on campus through the medium of the Steelpan. Through learning how to play the instrument, we hope to spread greater appreciation for Caribbean arts and culture among the general Princeton community.

Black Arts Company (BAC)

The Black Arts Company is Princeton's premier hip-hop dance company. They strive to maintain ties to the Black arts and incite cultural dialogue on movement from the diaspora.

Black Student-Athlete Collective (BSAC)

The Black Student-Athlete Collective is working actively to build relationships among students, coaches, Princeton Athletics Department staff, and the wider campus community; create networks and spaces in which current black student-athletes can interact with one another and alumni; and promote, create, and assist efforts to increase diversity amongst students, coaches, and staff.
